Title: Understanding Oregon's Recidivism: Insights from the November 2022 Report

Abstract:

This paper provides a summary of the Oregon Recidivism Analysis report released in November 2022 by the Oregon Criminal Justice Commission (CJC). The report, a collaborative effort with state agencies, analyzes recidivism rates in Oregon, focusing on individuals under post-prison supervision and those on new probation sentences. Key findings highlight declining rates of arrest, conviction, and incarceration recidivism, approaching historic lows. The impact of legislative changes, particularly Measure 110 decriminalizing drug possession, is also explored. The methodology ensures reliable data collection from multiple sources, aiding evidence-based decision-making.

Introduction:

Recidivism, the re-offending of individuals previously involved in the criminal justice system, is a significant concern in Oregon. The November 2022 report from the CJC, the sixteenth in its series, offers insights into recidivism trends in the state. This paper summarizes the report's findings, focusing on trends, methodology, and the impact of legislative changes.

Recidivism Trends:

The report reveals decreasing rates of arrest, conviction, and incarceration recidivism among individuals under post-prison supervision. Similar trends are observed for those on probation, with a slight increase in incarceration recidivism in the most recent cohort. These trends suggest progress in reducing recidivism rates in Oregon. The impact of Measure 110, which decriminalized drug possession, is noted as a contributing factor to these trends.

Methodology:

The report employs a robust methodology to track recidivism rates, utilizing data from the Oregon Department of Corrections, Oregon Judicial Department, and Oregon State Police. Individuals are considered recidivists if they are incarcerated for a new crime, or arrested or convicted within three years of release from prison or starting probation. This methodology ensures the reliability of recidivism assessments.

Impact of Measure 110:

Measure 110 significantly influences recidivism rates by decriminalizing most instances of drug possession. The report provides historical recidivism rates excluding drug possession events for comparative analysis. This legislative change has implications for understanding recidivism dynamics in Oregon.

Conclusion:

In conclusion, the Oregon Recidivism Analysis report offers valuable insights into recidivism trends in the state. Policymakers, practitioners, and stakeholders can use these insights for evidence-based decision-making. The full report is available for further reference.
