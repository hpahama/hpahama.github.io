Response Paper: Dr. Nicki Washington’s Concerns on SIGCSE Reviews and DEI

Author and Context:

Dr. Nicki Washington recently expressed her concerns via email regarding the reviews received for a panel submission to the 2023 Special Interest Group in CS Education (SIGCSE) Technical Symposium. In her communication, she delves into critical issues related to the peer-review process and highlights the problematic nature of the SIGCSE community, particularly concerning diversity, equity, and inclusion (DEI).

Key Points Raised by Dr. Washington:

  • Lack of Diversity: Dr. Washington draws attention to the glaring lack of diversity within the SIGCSE community, specifically focusing on race and educational backgrounds. This deficiency undermines the community’s ability to foster a truly inclusive environment.

    "Many of us have discussed how problematic the community is, how it’s dominated by white folks..." - Dr. Nicki Washington

  • Critique of Reviews: She criticizes the reviews received for the panel submission, characterizing them as being rooted in whiteness, elitism, and ignorance. Dr. Washington questions when there will be a collective conversation addressing these deeply ingrained issues.

    "However, when I read reviews that are rooted in whiteness, elitism, and ignorance, then I have to ask the question: 'When will we have the collective conversation about SIGCSE...'" - Dr. Nicki Washington

  • Specific Excerpts from Reviews: Dr. Washington shares specific excerpts from the reviews, addressing various concerns. These include relevance, structural considerations for hybrid audience participation, panelist qualifications, feedback on grammar, and the overall evaluation.

  • Highlighting Contradictions and Inconsistencies: She meticulously points out contradictions and inconsistencies in the reviewers’ feedback. Instances of elitism, ignorance, and disrespect emerge, revealing the need for a more thoughtful and informed review process.

    "All of the comments from our proposal review demonstrate blatant elitism, ignorance, and more from the reviewers." - Dr. Nicki Washington

  • Disappointment with SIGCSE Leadership: Dr. Washington expresses her disappointment with the SIGCSE leadership for allowing such problematic reviews to go unchallenged and unsupported. She emphasizes the importance of leadership taking a proactive stance in promoting inclusivity and fairness.

    "I would expect a program committee to do a much better job of protecting scholars of all backgrounds and positions..." - Dr. Nicki Washington

Concerns Raised in Reviews:

The reviewers’ criticisms centered on several aspects of the panel submission:

  • Clarity and Relevance
  • Structure for Hybrid Audience Participation
  • Panelist Qualifications
  • Grammar Issues

My Personal Impressions and Opinions:

In reading Dr. Washington's critique of the SIGCSE reviews and the DEI issues within the community, it's evident that she presents a compelling argument for change. Her passion for diversity and inclusion in academia is palpable, and her willingness to challenge the status quo is commendable. However, the language used in her message gives the impression of extreme frustration and disillusionment. While her concerns are valid, the intensity of her words may alienate some readers and detract from the overall message. Nonetheless, her message serves as a crucial catalyst for much-needed conversations and actions toward a more equitable academic environment.

Call to Action:

In conclusion, Dr. Nicki Washington’s email serves as a poignant reminder of the pressing need for change within the SIGCSE community. Her impassioned critique sheds light on systemic issues of diversity, equity, and inclusion that cannot be ignored. While her message may evoke feelings of discomfort or defensiveness, it also presents a valuable opportunity for self-reflection and growth. Now is the time for the SIGCSE community to actively engage in difficult conversations, challenge entrenched biases, and implement tangible measures to create a more inclusive and equitable environment for all scholars. By embracing diversity and amplifying marginalized voices, we can cultivate a community where every individual feels valued, respected, and empowered to thrive.
